About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Institut Denis Poisson, specializing in Bayesian approaches and inverse problems in imaging. My research focuses on combining data-driven priors with Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods to solve complex imaging problems. I am passionate about interdisciplinary applications, particularly in medical imaging and image restoration.

Outside of research, I enjoy engaging with the scientific community through seminars and workshops. I am also an avid learner, constantly exploring new techniques in deep learning and uncertainty quantification.

Research

Inverse Problems in Imaging

My research focuses on solving inverse problems in imaging by combining data-driven priors with Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods to reconstruct unknown images from its degraded and noisy versions.

Bayesian Approaches

Development of Bayesian inference methods for solving inverse problems, incorporating prior knowledge through probabilistic models to achieve robust parameter estimation.

Interdisciplinary Applications

Applications in image restoration, medical imaging:

  • • Denoising and deblurring
  • • Inpainting and super-resolution
  • • Positron emission tomography
