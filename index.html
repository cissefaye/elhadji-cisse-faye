About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Institut Denis Poisson, working on Bayesian approaches to inverse problems in imaging. My research combines data-driven priors with Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to solve challenging reconstruction tasks. I am particularly interested in uncertainty quantification and interdisciplinary applications in medical imaging and image restoration.

I regularly engage with the scientific community through seminars and workshops and continuously explore recent techniques in deep learning and probabilistic modeling.

Download CV

Research

Inverse Problems in Imaging

I study inverse problems in imaging, combining data-driven priors with MCMC methods to reconstruct unknown images from degraded and noisy measurements.

Bayesian Approaches

I develop Bayesian inference methods for inverse problems, leveraging prior knowledge through probabilistic models to obtain robust estimators and credible uncertainty quantification.

Interdisciplinary Applications

Applications include image restoration and medical imaging:

  • Denoising and deblurring
  • Inpainting and super-resolution
  • Positron emission tomography
